geoPaul is...

Hi, my name is Paul. Since 2009 I've considered myself a geographer. That was the year I entered a M.S. program at Michigan State University. I completed my master's in 2011, and in 2012 began a PhD program at Indiana University.

In 2017, after conducting fieldwork in several locations in Kenya and successfully defending my disseration, I completed my PhD program.

Today I am a GIS developer at Wayne County, Michigan. I manage GIS software, and, primarily, I build GIS applications to improve the public's access to resources and information.

My journey from academia to the public sector has exposed me to a range of learning opportunities and GIS challenges. I've equipped my GIS toolbelt with knowledge of proprietary and open source GIS software, built geoprocessing widgets to be used in client-side analysis, and deployed web GIS environments for the purposes of hosting numerous custom built map applications.

As my career path and skills have evolved, I've increasingly involved myself in more coding and development projects. And, to my surprise, I've found that I really enjoy this evolution.
